History of Computer Programming

From 200 BC to 2000

Here's a history of devices and inventions:

  • 200 BC to 70 BC - Unknown exact date, the Antikythera mechanism is the oldest example of an analog computer. It was used by the Greeks to predict astronomical positions and eclipses decades in advance.
  • 850 - The book of ingenious devices created by the Banu Musa at the House of Wisdom, is a large illustrated work on mechanical devices. One of them was an automatic flute player which is possibly the first programmable machine.
  • 1206 - Ismail Al-Jazari of the Artuqid State is known for his elephant clock invention which is useful for recording the hours to match uneven lengths in days. It makes use of of a humanoid robot and a mechanical bird which is why he's know as the father of robotics.
  • ~1670 - One of the first computer scientists, Gottfried Leibniz worked on the the calculus ratiocinator as well as developments on first-order logic which were foundations of computer science.
  • 1837 - The analyitical engine invented by Charles Babbage is an improvement on his previous work, the difference engine. It was a general purpose computer that was able to have control flow, conditionals, loops, a logic unit, and memory.
  • 1852 - Building off of Babbage's analyitical engine, Ada Lovelace proposed that it could be used for more than calculations, it could be a program solving complex problems.
  • 1936 - The turing machine was invented and it became the foundation of computing. Using a memory tape, it was capable of arbitrary computation and was able to solve the Entscheidungsproblem.
  • 1947 - The first assembly language, ARC and it was written by Kathleen Booth. The assembly code was converted to executable machine code called the assembler.
  • 1969 - The first Unix operating system was designed by Ken Thompson at Bell Labs. Furthermore, he invented the B programming language which was a direct predecessor of the C language. It was a typeless language so the only data types that could be used was the machines memory. Later on he developed the C language which allowed user-defined types.
  • 1979 - C with classes was invented by Bjarne Stroustrup, later on named C++. It had OOP, constructors, destructors, and built-in and user-defined types.
  • 1989 - The world wide web was invented and the first HTTP communcation between client and server was sent. Tim Berners-Lee had the idea of combining hypertext with TCP and DNS to get the WWW.
  • 2000 - In the late 1990s and early 2000s came computer virusus and in May 5th 2000, the love bug worm was sent as a social engineering attack affecting millions of computers worldwide.
